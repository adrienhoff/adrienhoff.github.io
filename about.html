ABOUT ME

I’m a GIS professional with deep experience designing, building, and maintaining spatial systems for public safety and emergency management. My background spans enterprise geodatabases, ArcGIS Enterprise and Online, Python automation, and real-time data workflows, with a focus on making complex systems reliable under real-world pressure.

I’ve spent much of my career working in 911 and fire environments, where data accuracy, uptime, and clarity are not optional. I specialize in integrating disparate data sources, automating geospatial processes, and translating operational needs into scalable GIS solutions. My work often lives at the intersection of GIS, backend services, and infrastructure, whether that’s syncing external feeds, publishing services, managing upgrades, or supporting mission-critical applications like TAK and web-based mapping tools.

I hold a Master’s degree in Geographic Information Sciences from the University of Denver and currently work as a Spatial Technologies Project Manager with the State of Colorado, leading GIS research and innovation initiatives in support of wildfire and emergency response operations.

This portfolio highlights selected projects that demonstrate my approach to problem-solving, system design, and applied GIS. I care about clean data, thoughtful architecture, and building tools that people actually trust when it matters.
